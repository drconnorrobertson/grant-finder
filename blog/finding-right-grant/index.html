How to Find the Right Grant for Your Nonprofit

By Dr. Connor Robertson, Founder of GrantFinder · February 7, 2026 · 5 min read

Not all grants are created equal, and applying for the wrong ones wastes your most valuable resource: time. As the founder of GrantFinder, I built this platform specifically to solve the discovery problem. But even with the right tools, knowing how to evaluate and prioritize opportunities is essential. Here is a practical framework for finding grants that actually fit your organization.

Start with Self-Assessment

Before you search for grants, get clear on your own organization's strengths, programs, and capacity. What programs are fully developed and ready for outside investment? What geographic areas do you serve? What populations do you work with? Having crisp answers to these questions will make your search far more efficient.

Many nonprofits cast too wide a net, applying for anything that seems remotely related. This scattershot approach leads to weak proposals and burnout. A focused search strategy yields better results with less effort.

Understand Funder Priorities

Every funder has specific priorities, even when their stated mission seems broad. Research recent awards to understand who they actually fund, not just who they say they fund. Look at the size of past grants, the types of organizations that received them, and the geographic focus. This intelligence helps you assess whether your organization is genuinely competitive for a given opportunity.

Use Filters Strategically

On GrantFinder's search page, you can filter by category, state, funding amount, and deadline. Start with your strongest program area and your geographic footprint. Then narrow by funding range to find grants that match your program scale. A $5,000 grant requires a very different proposal than a $500,000 one, and your time investment should reflect that.

Evaluate Fit Before You Apply

For each potential grant, ask yourself three questions. First, does this funder's mission align with our work? Second, do we meet all eligibility requirements? Third, can we realistically complete the application by the deadline with a strong submission? If the answer to any of these is no, move on. There will always be more opportunities.

Build a Grant Calendar

The most organized nonprofits maintain a rolling calendar of grant deadlines and reporting requirements. This prevents last-minute scrambles and ensures you never miss a renewal opportunity. Many grants from the same funder recur annually, so tracking past applications helps you plan ahead.

Leverage GrantFinder's Data

We pull data daily from Grants.gov, state portals, and foundation databases so you always see the most current opportunities. Bookmark grants you are interested in, check back regularly for new listings, and explore our category pages for curated collections in your focus area. The more efficiently you can identify strong-fit grants, the more time you have to write compelling proposals.

Finding the right grant is the first step. Winning it requires a strong proposal, a realistic budget, and a clear vision for impact. For tips on the writing process, check out our guide on writing winning grant proposals.
